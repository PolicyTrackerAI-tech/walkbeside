# Naming sprint — keep "Honest Funeral" or rename? (2026-07-15)

**Why this exists:** `honestfuneral.com` is squatted by an adult site. GoDaddy
brokerage failed; the owner is unreachable. A hospice administrator or a
grieving family member who hears the name and types `.com` lands on porn —
the single worst trust failure available to this brand. The live site is
`honestfuneral.co`. Decision checkpoint: **Sat Jul 25**
(`PRODUCT_SPRINT_2026-07-16.md` D12/§7.7).

Everything below is point-in-time research from 2026-07-15: domain
availability verified against the registries themselves (Verisign/PIR RDAP),
collisions via best-effort web research. **None of this is legal trademark
clearance** — before committing to any name, run a real TESS + state +
common-law knockout search (counsel, ~days).

---

## 1. Criteria a winning name must pass

1. `.com` **and** `.co/.org/.net` unregistered (the whole point — no
   squatters, ever again).
2. Says or evokes what we are (honest dealing at the end of life) without
   being morbid; survives being spoken aloud at a hospice front desk and
   typed from memory.
3. Works in the two sentences the business lives on: *"Provided free to your
   family by [Hospice] through ___"* and *"___ is the free, neutral source
   of truth for funeral pricing."*
4. Doesn't collide with death-care incumbents (Lantern, Empathy, Cake,
   Farewill, Parting, Funeralocity…), a funeral home (fatal for neutrality),
   or an aggressive trademark enforcer.
5. Grows past funerals (hospice → employer benefit) without renaming again.

## 4. The decision, framed honestly

**Option A — keep the name.** Register `honestfuneralguide.com` +
`gethonestfuneral.com` (both available) as defensive redirects, put the
`.co` on every card, and accept that some fraction of spoken/typed-from-
memory traffic hits porn forever, including hospice procurement officers
mid-evaluation. Cost: ~$25/yr. Risk: permanent, unfixable, lands on exactly
the moment (first impression, grief, B2B diligence) the brand can least
afford.

**Option B — rename now.** Traffic and brand equity are near their lifetime
minimum; the B2B2C repositioning (sprint D11) rewrites much of the site copy
anyway, and `lib/brand.ts` ships Wednesday so the name becomes a one-file
text change. This is the cheapest week there will ever be to do it. Cost:
one execution day next sprint + new collateral + Search Console
change-of-address. Risk: losing what little name recognition exists (one
pilot conversation, early SEO).

**Recommendation: Option B — rename, to `Open Farewell`** (openfarewell.com
/ .co / .org / .net all free), with **Plain Farewell** as the equal-quality
alternate if it reads better to you aloud, and **Wise Farewell** third (its
funeral-home surname collision is the tiebreak against it). The two
Honest-preserving candidates both fail the buyer channel (Passages fraud
echo; sendoff.co is a literal funeral home). "Open" carries the entire
thesis — open pricing, open methodology, open data — and *Provided free by
[Hospice] through Open Farewell* reads naturally.

**Do this regardless of the final pick, today if possible:** defensively
register the full stacks for the top TWO candidates (~$100 total for 8
domains). RDAP-available means available *this minute* — candidate names in
a pushed GitHub doc are exactly the kind of thing domain-tasting bots
register. Buying is yours to do (registrar of your choice, WHOIS privacy
on); nothing here commits you to the rename.

## 5. If the answer is rename — execution inventory (one day, next sprint)

1. **Domains:** buy the stack; point `.com` at Vercel; 301 the rest.
2. **Old domain lives forever:** `honestfuneral.co` 301s page-for-page to
   the new domain — *except* inbound email. In-flight negotiations carry
   `reply+<hash>@` reply-to addresses on the current domain: **Postmark
   inbound stays live on the old domain indefinitely**, new cases mint
   reply-tos on the new one.
3. **Email:** Google Workspace — add the new domain, recreate the 9 aliases,
   flip the primary when DNS settles; Resend — verify the new sending
   domain (DKIM/SPF, allow 24–72h), update from-addresses in
   `lib/email.ts`/env.
4. **App:** `NEXT_PUBLIC_APP_URL` + Vercel domain config; `lib/brand.ts`
   name constant (ships Wed Jul 22) carries every user-facing name string;
   grep pass for the remaining hardcoded "Honest Funeral"/"honestfuneral.co"
   (OG images in `lib/og.ts`, print letterheads, `app/sitemap.ts`,
   JSON-LD, email templates, `docs/sales/*`).
5. **Search:** Google Search Console — verify new domain, submit
   change-of-address; resubmit sitemap; canonical tags flip with
   `NEXT_PUBLIC_APP_URL`.
6. **Ops/legal (yours):** registrar auto-renew ON for the whole stack;
   social handles (grab on X/IG/LinkedIn/FB same day); LLC d/b/a filing per
   counsel; Stripe account display name before billing goes live (sprint
   D1); update the pilot conversation and any sent collateral.
7. **Verify:** smoke-check on the new domain, old-domain redirects, an
   end-to-end inbound-email reply on an in-flight case, magic-link auth
   redirect allowlist (`/auth/callback` origin check), Postmark webhook
   Basic-auth URL.
