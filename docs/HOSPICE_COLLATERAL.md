# Hospice collateral — the materials a hospice hands families (+ what you hand the hospice)

The B2B2C marketing materials, with **draft copy you can use**. Two audiences:
the **hospice** (to get a yes and equip them) and the **family** (the neutral
resource the hospice hands over — free to them, because the hospice is the
customer). Companion to [`HOSPICE_GTM.md`](HOSPICE_GTM.md) and
[`GO_TO_MARKET.md`](GO_TO_MARKET.md).

---

## 0. Anti-steering rules for ALL collateral (read first — this is law)

Guardrail #3 and the reason a hospice can legally endorse us
([`COMPLIANCE_ADDENDUM.md`](COMPLIANCE_ADDENDUM.md)):

- **Never name or recommend a specific funeral home** in any material.
- The hospice **hands the family a neutral resource and says "here are your
  options"** — never "use this home." The family always chooses.
- Approved framing: *"a free, neutral guide to funeral costs and your options."*
  Banned framing: anything that reads as a referral to a particular provider.
- **One approved handoff line** (the single reviewed constant, reused everywhere
  — the partner portal and compliance docs point at this):
  > "Here's a free, neutral guide to funeral costs and your rights — from a
  > group that takes no money from funeral homes. It's yours to use however
  > helps; you decide everything."

## 1. Family one-pager — the hospice hands this to the family

**Use:** printed leave-behind (and a PDF) the bereavement coordinator gives a
family. Co-branded "[Hospice] + Honest Funeral." Half the page is a short URL +
QR + claim code so they can self-enroll (no PHI to us).

**Draft copy:**

> ### Help with funeral costs — free, and on your side
>
> [Hospice Name] has partnered with **Honest Funeral** so you don't have to face
> funeral pricing alone. It's **free to you** — and we take **no money from
> funeral homes**, so we have no reason to point you anywhere but where's right
> for your family.
>
> **What you get, free:**
> - **Fair prices for your area** — what a funeral *should* cost near you, by
>   line item, so you know if a quote is reasonable.
> - **"Is this quote fair?"** — send us a price list and we'll flag the
>   overcharges and the items you can legally decline.
> - **A real person on your side** — we can contact funeral homes for you, gather
>   itemized quotes, and lay your options side by side. You choose. Always.
>
> **Start here:** honestfuneral.co  ·  [QR]  ·  Your code: **[CLAIM CODE]**
>
> There's no rush and no wrong choice. Take what helps, leave the rest.

*Voice: calm, plain, no urgency, no sales. Reassurance over feature-listing.*

## 1.5. For the coordinator — the moment of handing this over

**Use:** internal reassurance for the coordinator themselves, not shown to the
family. This is what makes someone actually do this in the moment, rather than
filing the one-pager in a drawer.

**The moment feels bigger than it is.** You've just told a family someone
died. Handing over something about funeral costs can feel, right then, like
piling on. It isn't. A family this early is often relieved to have one
concrete thing to hold onto when a decision this large is coming — you're not
raising money, you're taking one thing off their plate.

**Why it's fine to say, right here:** you're not selling anything, steering
them anywhere, or asking for anything back. It's the same free, neutral
resource described in the approved hand-off line above, word for word. Give it
the way you'd give any other after-death resource — no pitch, no follow-up
required from you.

**It's legal and it's yours to give.** Honest Funeral takes no money from
funeral homes or insurers and never recommends a specific one — that's what
makes this different from a referral, and why anti-steering rules don't apply
to it (see [`COMPLIANCE_ADDENDUM.md`](COMPLIANCE_ADDENDUM.md)). You're not
vouching for an outcome; you're handing over a tool the family uses however
helps, entirely on their own terms.

**A script, if you want one:**
> "Here's something a lot of families find useful for the funeral-costs part —
> it's free, and they're not paid by any funeral home, so there's no catch.
> Totally up to you whether you use it."

No follow-up, no tracking who used it, no individual reporting — just
something good to have said.

## 3. Hospice leave-behind — what you give the bereavement coordinator

**Use:** the one-pager you leave after the pitch; the thing that gets the *yes*
and that the coordinator references. Lead with **their** pain, not our features.

**Draft copy:**

> ### Meet your 13-month bereavement mandate — without adding staff hours
>
> Medicare requires ~13 months of bereavement support after every patient death
> (42 CFR 418.64), and you're paid nothing extra to do it — and it shows up in
> your **CAHPS** family-satisfaction scores. Honest Funeral handles the part your
> counselors aren't resourced for: **the funeral-pricing and after-death maze.**
>
> **What your families get (free to them):** a neutral guide to fair funeral
> prices, a "is this quote fair?" check, and an advocate who gathers itemized
> quotes and lays options side by side — they choose.
>
> **What you get:** a report — families served, satisfaction, savings,
> time-to-resolution — for your CAHPS and compliance file. Proof you delivered.
>
> **Why you can put this in a grieving family's hands:** we take **no money from
> funeral homes or insurers**, and we **never steer** a family to a specific
> home. We present neutral options; the family decides. That's why it's ethical —
> and legal — for you to hand it to them.
>
> **The ask:** a free 60-day pilot with ~10–15 families. If the satisfaction and
> savings numbers prove out, we talk about a simple annual agreement. No risk to
> start.

>   a simple per-facility annual agreement. If not, you owe nothing.
> - **Data:** de-identified outcomes only; family data stays private to the
>   family; no PHI required (families self-enroll).
