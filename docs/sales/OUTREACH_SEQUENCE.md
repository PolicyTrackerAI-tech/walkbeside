# Hospice Cold Outreach Sequence — 3 Touches to a Discovery Call

> **What this is.** A copy-paste cold sequence to get a Utah hospice to book a 20-minute discovery call. Four pieces: **Email 1** (open), a **Call opener + Voicemail**, **Email 2** (follow-up), and a **LinkedIn connection note**. Fill in every `[bracketed field]` before sending. Lead with *their* pain — referral reputation and the unfunded 13-month bereavement mandate — never with our features.
>
> **Companion docs:** voice and full playbook in [`HOSPICE_GTM.md`](../HOSPICE_GTM.md); approved family-facing copy and anti-steering rules in [`HOSPICE_COLLATERAL.md`](../HOSPICE_COLLATERAL.md). The single milestone these touches serve: a **booked discovery call**.

---

## Who to send to

Three buyer roles — start with whichever you can reach; the ED ultimately signs:

1. **Bereavement / Grief Coordinator** (or Director of Counseling) — **the champion.** Owns the 42 CFR 418.64 burden day-to-day. Send **Email 1** here first.
2. **Executive Director / Administrator** — **the economic buyer.** Signs the pilot and the contract. Lead the *referral-reputation* argument hardest with this person.
3. **Community-Liaison / Marketing / Outreach Director** — **owns the referral-reputation case** (census from hospital/SNF/physician goodwill). The cleanest fit for the "this protects your referral sources" angle.

**Warm path (always try first):** a **chaplain or social worker** you can reach is the fastest human — they feel the family's funeral-and-paperwork panic firsthand. Ask them for a warm intro to the bereavement coordinator rather than cold-emailing the ED.

> **Sequencing.** One contact per hospice at a time. Email 1 → Day 0. Call + voicemail → Day 2–3 (if no reply). Email 2 → Day 5–6. LinkedIn note → Day 7, or anytime as a parallel warm touch. If no reply after the full sequence, mark dead and move on.

---

## CAN-SPAM checklist (every email send)

- ☐ Truthful subject line and "from" name — you are clearly identified as a real person at Honest Funeral.
- ☐ **Valid physical postal address** in the footer.
- ☐ A working **opt-out** ("reply STOP and I won't write again") — honored within 10 days.
- ☐ Personalized to *this* hospice (their name, their referral context, their region). No mail-merge tells.

**Subject options (pick one):**

- The funeral-pricing maze your bereavement program isn't funded for
- Protecting [Hospice]'s reputation through the after-death weeks
- [Hospice]'s emotional-and-spiritual-support score — and the families behind it *(use when you've pulled their Care Compare sub-score)*

> I'm reaching out to [Hospice] because the part that quietly shapes your reputation isn't the care before the death — it's what happens after. A family that gets overcharged or lost in the paperwork remembers it, and that's the story that travels back to the hospitals, SNFs, and physicians who send you referrals. >
> Worth 20 minutes to see if it'd help your families? I'm in Utah and happy to come to you.
>
> [Your name] · Honest Funeral · [phone] · honestfuneral.co
> [Physical postal address] · Reply "stop" and I won't write again.

## Call opener + voicemail (Day 2–3)

**Call opener (15–20 sec — for the bereavement coordinator or front desk):**

> "Hi, is this [First name]? It's [Your name] with Honest Funeral — I'll be quick. We give grieving families a free, neutral guide through funeral pricing and the after-death paperwork, and we hand the hospice a report that proves the families were supported. The reason I'm calling [Hospice] specifically: a bad funeral experience is the thing families remember and pass back to your referral sources — and we take no money from funeral homes, so we're something you can actually put in a family's hands. Could I grab 20 minutes this week to see if it'd help your families?"

> *If "we already do bereavement":* "Totally — your counselors do the grief support, and that's not what we touch. We handle the **funeral-pricing and after-death admin maze** they aren't staffed for, and we give you the documented proof. We complement your program, we don't replace it."

**Voicemail (15 sec):**

> "Hi [First name], it's [Your name] with Honest Funeral. I help hospices give families a calm, neutral path through funeral pricing and the after-death paperwork — free to the family, no money from funeral homes — which protects the experience families carry back to your referral sources. I'll send a short note too; I'd love 20 minutes. My number is [phone]. Thanks."
