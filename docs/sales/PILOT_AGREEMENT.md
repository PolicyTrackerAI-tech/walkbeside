## Why we're doing this

The Hospice is federally required to provide roughly 13 months of bereavement support after each patient death (42 CFR 418.64), and is paid nothing extra to do it. Honest Funeral handles the part a bereavement team usually isn't resourced for: **the funeral-pricing and after-death paperwork maze.** This pilot is a free, time-boxed way for the Hospice to see whether that helps its families — and to measure it — before anyone discusses paying for it.

---

## What we're testing (scope)

- Honest Funeral provides **neutral funeral-pricing advocacy and light after-death navigation** (an after-death checklist, what-to-do-next guidance, and resource pointers) to approximately **[10–15] of the Hospice's families** over the pilot term, **free to those families**.
- **Families enroll themselves.** The Hospice hands a family a link, card, or code; the family chooses whether to sign up directly with Honest Funeral. The Hospice is **not** asked to share any patient health information (PHI) with Honest Funeral as part of this pilot (see *Privacy*, below).
- Honest Funeral runs **every pilot case by hand** and stays in direct contact with the family it serves.
- This is an evaluation, not a service contract: there is **no fee, no minimum, and no exclusivity** for either side.

---

## Neutrality and anti-steering (the part that makes this OK to hand a family)

- Honest Funeral takes **no money from funeral homes or insurers** — ever.
- Honest Funeral **never steers** a family to any specific funeral home. We present neutral options and itemized prices; **the family decides everything.**
- Honest Funeral makes **no paid referrals** and receives **no commission, kickback, or consideration** from any funeral provider in connection with a family.
- The resource is offered to families **after admission only**. Neither party will use it, or refer to it, in the Hospice's pre-admission marketing or to encourage the selection of the Hospice.

This is why the Hospice can ethically and lawfully put the resource in a grieving family's hands.

---

## Privacy

- Because families **self-enroll**, the Hospice is **not required to share PHI** with Honest Funeral for this pilot.
- The Hospice receives **aggregate, de-identified outcomes only** — never any individual family's private case details.
- If, during or after the pilot, the parties ever decide the Hospice will share PHI, a **Business Associate Agreement (BAA)** will be signed **before** any such information is exchanged. A short security summary is available on request.

---

## Use of outcomes data

The Hospice grants Honest Funeral a perpetual permission to use **de-identified, aggregate** outcomes from the pilot (e.g., average savings, satisfaction, time-to-resolution) to improve its service, report results, and include in Honest Funeral's **aggregate public pricing benchmarks and datasets** (such as a published fair-price index). **No individual family's identity or private details** are included, no figure traceable to the Hospice or to any family is published, and the Hospice's name will not be used in any public material without its written permission.

The de-identified aggregate dataset Honest Funeral builds from outcomes across all of its partners remains **Honest Funeral's property**; nothing in this pilot gives either party ownership of the other's underlying data or methods.

---

## Term, cost, and obligation

- **Term:** 60 days from the date both parties sign.
- **Cost:** **$0.** The pilot is free to the Hospice and free to its families.
- **No obligation:** Neither party is obligated to enter a paid agreement afterward. Either party may end the pilot at any time with written notice (email is fine).
- **What happens next:** If the numbers prove out, the parties may discuss a simple annual or per-family arrangement — **paid by the Hospice only**, never by a family and never by a funeral home or insurer. If they don't, we shake hands and the Hospice owes nothing.

---

## Confidentiality (light, mutual)

Each party will keep the other's non-public business information (pricing discussions, methodology, internal operations) confidential and use it only for the purpose of this pilot. This does not cover the de-identified, aggregate outcomes described above, or information either party already knew or that becomes public through no fault of theirs.
