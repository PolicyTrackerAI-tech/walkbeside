## 1. The one-paragraph business

Honest Funeral is a **consumer-advocate web app** for families who have just
had a death in the family. It is explicitly on the family's side, takes **no
money from funeral homes**, and is not itself a funeral home, broker,
directory, insurer, or law firm. Free tools help families understand fair
funeral pricing and their rights. One **paid** feature ("advocate outreach")
contacts funeral homes on the family's behalf — transparently, as the family's
named advocate — to collect itemized price quotes and present them side by
side. We charge a **flat $49**, only when a family chooses a home we presented.

---

## 2. What the product actually does (surfaces)

**Free, no account, no PII collected:**
- **Fair-price lookup** — enter a ZIP, see national-benchmark price ranges per
  funeral line item, adjusted regionally. No data saved.
- **Prep kit / rights guide** — questions to ask, upsells to decline, FTC
  Funeral Rule rights, decline scripts. Static content.
- **Decide flow** — answers a few questions, recommends a service type.
- **Faith guides** — per-tradition funeral norms (informational).
- **Obituary helper** — AI drafts an obituary from family inputs.
- **Price-list analyzer** — family uploads a photo of a funeral home's price
  list; AI flags line items above the fair range.
- **Post-funeral checklist, worksheet, veterans-benefits checker** — guidance.

**Paid — "advocate outreach" (flat $49):**
1. Family creates an account and authorizes us to contact homes on their behalf.
2. We email funeral homes in the family's area **identifying ourselves as
   Honest Funeral, the family's named advocate** (we do **not** impersonate the
   family and do **not** send anonymous/secret-shopper inquiries — see §4).
3. We invoke the family's right under the **FTC Funeral Rule** to receive an
   itemized General Price List (GPL).
4. Homes reply; we collect/parse quotes and present them side by side.
5. Family picks a home. We release contact info. **Stripe charges $49.**
6. Refund offered within 14 days if the chosen home won't honor its written quote.

**Secondary revenue [CONFIRM — planned, may not be live]:** at deal close we may
show referrals (insurance, funeral financing via credit unions, estate
attorneys), disclosed on the same screen. We'd earn a referral fee.

## 3. How money moves (exact)

- **Who pays:** the family (consumer). **Never** the funeral home.
- **How much:** flat **$49**, one time, per family.
- **When charged:** only at the moment the family selects a home we presented
  through advocate outreach. Free tools never charge.
- **Processor:** Stripe. Payment method may be collected up front; charge fires
  on selection. [CONFIRM current Stripe flow: authorize-then-capture vs charge.]
- **Refunds:** $49 refundable within 14 days if the selected home refuses to
  honor its quote.
- **No commissions, kickbacks, or listing fees from funeral homes. We do not
  hold or transmit any funeral payment** — the family pays the funeral home
  directly at the home's own quoted price.

## 4. Critical factual representations we make (counsel should pressure-test these against reality)

These are claims the **site itself makes**. If any is not literally true of the
shipped product, that is a deception risk we need to fix before launch:

1. **"We identify ourselves by name in every outreach email; we do not
   impersonate families; outreach is transparent, not anonymous."** → Outreach
   emails are built from a **static, deterministic template**
   (`lib/negotiation/email-body.ts`, sent by `app/api/negotiate/start/route.ts`),
   not generated by AI — so this representation is guaranteed by construction.
   The send path also enforces a code-level **email denylist** and an
   `OUTREACH_LIVE` **kill-switch** (dry-run unless explicitly enabled). Counsel
   should read the template once and confirm wording. This is our single most
   important representation and our main defense against impersonation/deception
   claims.
2. **"We take no money from funeral homes."** → True today; keep true.
3. **Fair-price ranges / "overpay by $2,000–$5,000" / "save $1,500–$3,000."**
   → These are advertising claims that may require substantiation under FTC Act
   §5. Our price data is currently **national benchmarks, not yet validated
   against local price lists** (we corrected the code comments to say this).
   Question: what substantiation/disclaimers do we need?
4. **"Deal rating: good / fair / overpriced"** on specific homes → opinion vs
   factual claim about a named business; defamation/trade-libel exposure?
5. **Former claim now REMOVED:** the site previously said it was "built by a
   licensed funeral director." The funeral director is **not currently a
   founder or active participant**, so we removed all such claims to avoid a
   false-endorsement/deceptive-credential problem. Counsel should confirm the
   removal is complete and advise on what we may say if/when she joins.

## 5. Risk areas — for counsel to assess

### A. FTC Funeral Rule (16 CFR Part 453)
- The Rule binds **funeral providers**, not us. But our whole paid feature
  **invokes** it on consumers' behalf. **Q:** Any risk in a non-provider
  asserting consumers' Funeral Rule rights to homes? Any registration/standing
  issue? Can we accurately describe homes' obligations without liability if we
  get a nuance wrong?

### B. Funeral licensing / unauthorized practice
- Many states license funeral establishments and directors and regulate who may
  **arrange** funerals, **negotiate** on a family's behalf, or act as a funeral
  **broker**. We believe we are an information service + communications agent,
  **not** arranging dispositions or handling remains/payment. **Q (highest
  priority):** In which states, if any, does "contacting homes and collecting
  quotes as the family's paid advocate" require a funeral, broker, or similar
  license? This is multi-state — see §6.

### C. Agency / authorization
- We act "on behalf of" the family. **Q:** What authorization do we need from
  the family (written? specific scope?) before contacting homes in their name?
  Is our in-product authorization step sufficient? What's our liability for the
  quotes we relay or the advice we give?

### D. FTC Act §5 / state UDAP (unfair & deceptive practices)
- Our marketing claims (savings figures, "fair price," deal ratings, "only if
  we save you money"-style framing) need substantiation and accurate framing.
  **Q:** What can we claim, with what disclaimers, and what must we be able to
  prove?

### E. Email & outreach law
- Outreach emails go to **funeral homes** (B2B). **Q:** CAN-SPAM applicability
  (commercial vs transactional/relationship), required headers/opt-out, and
  whether the named-advocate framing changes anything. Any TCPA exposure if we
  ever add SMS/calls. Any anti-impersonation statutes implicated by acting "as
  the family's advocate."

### F. Data privacy & security
- We collect: family account info, the deceased's details (obituary inputs),
  uploaded price-list photos, situation/scenario data, and the homes dataset.
  This is **sensitive bereavement + financial data**. Stored in Supabase.
  **Q:** Obligations under CCPA/CPRA and other state privacy laws; whether any
  of this is "sensitive personal information"; required privacy disclosures;
  retention limits; breach obligations; whether deceased-person data triggers
  anything. Is our current Privacy Policy (`/privacy`) adequate?

### G. Fee model, refunds, taxes
- Flat $49 consumer service fee with a 14-day conditional refund. **Q:** Is this
  a "brokerage" or "referral" fee under any state law that would change our
  obligations? Sales/service tax on a digital service by state? Refund-policy
  disclosure requirements?

### H. Secondary referral revenue [if pursued]
- Insurance referrals may require **insurance producer licensing** or specific
  disclosures. Financing referrals may implicate lending/credit rules. Attorney
  referrals implicate bar referral rules. **Q:** What licensing/disclosure does
  each referral type require before we can earn on it?

### I. Reliance liability on price data
- Families make decisions on our "fair price" output. **Q:** Disclaimer
  language and structure to limit liability for data inaccuracy, given the data
  is not yet locally validated.

### J. Advice disclaimers / not legal-financial-medical advice
- We already disclaim this on `/terms`, `/faq`, and in the footer. **Q:** Are
  the disclaimers adequately worded and placed?

### K. Entity, IP, contracts
- LLC + EIN exist. Trademark "Honest Funeral" is descriptive-leaning (weaker
  mark). Prior brand "Funerose" being sunset. **Q (low urgency):** trademark
  clearance/strategy; ensure all contracts/Stripe/domains are in the LLC's name
  to preserve the liability shield.
