# The Wedge — the one thing we are building toward

_This is the north star. Every priority serves this. If a task doesn't sharpen the
wedge or the product that delivers it, it waits. Product first — build it fast, then
go sell it to hospices and employers for their people._

## The wedge

Honest Funeral is the **neutral price truth for the American funeral** — the one guide
paid by no one with a stake in the bill.

Every other player is conflicted: the comparison sites are paid by the funeral homes,
and the after-loss concierges (Empathy and its kind) are paid by insurers. Not one of
them can look a grieving family in the eye and say _"you're being overcharged — here's
by how much, and here's what to do about it."_ **That sentence is the entire company.**
We can say it because we take money from no one who profits from the price.

**What we own:** the narrow, deep, defensible seat of **funeral-price transparency and
advocacy** — free to every family, open to everyone (not gated behind an insurance
policy), and backed by real data.

**Why we win:** counter-positioning. The funded incumbents cannot copy us without
breaking their own business model — the funeral homes can't bite the hand that pays
them, and the insurer-paid platforms can't tell a family to spend less. The seat is
structurally theirs to lose and ours to take.

## The tip of the spear — the product

The **"is this quote fair?" checker.** Snap a photo of any funeral price list and get
instantly:
- **overcharge flags** — line by line,
- **FTC-violation flags** — where the home is breaking the Funeral Rule,
- **"you're paying $X above fair"** — the number that lands.

It's the family-trust hook, the AI-citation magnet, and the engine that builds our
proprietary dataset — all in one.

## What we do NOT do

We do **not** become a broad after-death / estate / grief platform. That's Empathy's
ocean and we drown in it. We stay **narrow and go deep** — funeral price, plus only a
light practical wrapper. **Depth on price beats breadth every time.**

## Who pays

The institutions that serve the dying and have no stake in the price — **hospices
first, then employers.** Never the funeral homes. Never the family. Never an insurer as
our payer.

## The win condition

Become the **cited neutral price standard — the Carfax / Kelley Blue Book of funerals**
— that families trust, journalists and AI assistants quote, and the giants eventually
have to plug into or pay to acquire. We compound a **conflict-free brand** and a
**proprietary outcomes dataset** into a moat no one paid by the industry can replicate.

## The one rule that protects all of it

**Never take a dollar from anyone with a stake in the funeral price.** It's our mission,
our moat, and our legal shield, all at once.
