> trust/positioning copy. Every claim here is sourced and defensible
> (**guardrail #4** — we never publish a number we can't defend). Where a
> competitor fact is approximate or dated, it's labeled as such. Do not

## The one-sentence wedge

**Everyone who claims to help a grieving family with the funeral is paid by
someone with a stake in the funeral price — the funeral home, an insurer, or the
family's own wallet. We are the only one paid by none of them.** Hospices pay us
to give their families a neutral guide; we take no money from funeral homes or
insurers, and we never charge the family — which is the *only* reason a hospice
can ethically put us in a grieving family's hands.

## Competitor deep-dives

### 1. Empathy — the one that matters

- **What they are:** an insurer-paid bereavement + estate-administration platform.
  AI tools (LifeVault) plus human "care managers" that help beneficiaries through
  the logistics of a death.
- **Who pays them (the conflict):** **life insurers.** They partner with **8 of
  the top 10 U.S. life carriers**, reach **~45M+ policyholders**, and touch
  roughly **1 in 5 U.S. life-insurance claims** (per Empathy, 2025–26). They've
  raised **~$162M total**, including a **$72M Series C (May 2025)** with insurer
  investors (MetLife, New York Life, Securian, Allianz, Munich Re, Aflac
  Ventures). *(Sourced; see Sources.)*
- **Why we win:** Empathy's customer is the **insurer**, and its job is
  beneficiary goodwill on the *payout* — not getting the **funeral bill** down.
  An insurer-funded player has no incentive to be the neutral funeral-price
  arbiter, and structurally **can't take that seat without breaking its own
  model.** We are paid by the hospice and take **no** insurer or funeral-home
  money — the neutral funeral-price advocate seat is empty, and it's ours.
- **One-line counter:** *"Empathy is the insurer's concierge. We're the family's
  advocate — paid by the hospice, not by anyone with a stake in the funeral price."*
- **Honesty guard:** Empathy is well-funded, genuinely useful, and growing into
  employers. Don't claim they're bad or that they "steer." The distinction is
  **who pays and what that aligns them to** — not competence. If they ever match
  neutrality at scale, the pivot is to become the **data layer** they plug into
  (risk register).

### 2. Trust & Will — estate docs, family-paid

- **What they are:** guided online wills/trusts (a SaaS document platform).
  Raised **$25M (Mar 2025)** incl. Northwestern Mutual and UBS.
- **Who pays them (the conflict):** the **consumer**, upfront — Will plans ~$199
  (individual), Trust plans ~$499, plus an annual membership. They charge the
  person planning.
- **Why we win:** different job and opposite payer. They're **pre-need estate
  documents**; we're **at-need funeral pricing + after-death navigation**, free to
  the family. A hospice can't hand a grieving family a paywall.
- **One-line counter:** *"Great for writing a will months early. We're for the
  72 hours after a death — and we're free to the family."*

### 3. FreeWill — estate docs, nonprofit-funded

- **What they are:** free online wills funded by a **B2B2C** model.
- **Who pays them (the conflict):** **nonprofits and financial institutions**
  (2,400+ partners) who pay to drive **charitable bequests** (they cite $14B+ in
  bequests generated). Aligned to giving, not funeral-price neutrality.
- **Why we win:** FreeWill is actually a *positive model precedent* (free to the
  user, a third party pays) — but it's the **wrong product** (wills, not funerals)
  and the **wrong payer** for our job (a giving channel, not a hospice). We borrow
  the structure, not the lane.
- **One-line counter:** *"FreeWill proves free-to-the-user works when someone else
  pays. We apply that to the funeral — with the hospice as the payer."*

### 4. The graveyard — Cake, Lantern, Everplans, and the family-paid planners

- **What they were:** consumer end-of-life planning apps (checklists, document
  vaults, "plan ahead" tools). Same vintage as Halolife, Grace, Tulip, Willing,
  Farewill.
- **Who paid them (the conflict that killed them):** mostly the **family** —
  once-in-a-lifetime, grief-state, zero-repeat: the worst acquisition surface in
  the economy. Then look at where they **landed on exit**, which is the whole
  thesis in one line:
  - **Cake → Foundation Partners Group** (2024) — *a funeral-home operator.*
  - **Everplans → Precoa** (reported, 2024) — *a preneed lead-gen service for funeral homes.*
  - **Lantern → Wellthy** (reported, 2024) — absorbed into a care-coordination benefit.
- **Why we win:** the consumer-paid death-planning model is a **proven
  graveyard**, and the survivors got **absorbed into funeral-money channels** —
  the exact conflict we're built to never have. We make the **family the
  beneficiary and the hospice the payer**, and we're structurally un-ownable by
  funeral money.
- **One-line counter:** *"Every consumer-paid death-planning app either died or got
  bought by funeral money. We don't charge the family and we never take funeral-
  home money — that's why we're still standing on the family's side."*

### 5. Funeral price-comparison sites — paid by the homes

- **What they are:** directories/marketplaces that list funeral homes and surface
  "prices."
- **Who pays them (the conflict):** the **funeral homes**, via listing/lead/
  placement fees. The homes being ranked are the customers.
- **Why we win:** a site paid by the homes it ranks **is** the conflict we exist
  to remove. We rank no one for money; every published figure clears **n>5 +
  significance** with a public methodology (**guardrail #4**); we present a neutral
  side-by-side and **never steer** (**guardrail #3**).
- **One-line counter:** *"If the funeral homes pay for the ranking, it isn't
  neutral. We take no money from any home — that's the whole point."*

### 6. Funeral homes & the FTC-Rule status quo — the real incumbent

- **What it is:** the default. The home sets the price; the **FTC Funeral Rule**
  requires the itemized General Price List **in person**, and price information **by phone** — but there's
  **no requirement to post prices online** (only ~18% do), and the family is
  negotiating alone, in grief.
- **Who pays:** the **family**, at the worst moment, with the seller holding all
  the information.
- **Why we win:** we **invoke the family's FTC right for them**, gather itemized
  prices from local **vetted** homes, and return a **neutral side-by-side** — so
  the family decides with the seller's own numbers in hand. We turn the Rule's
  paper right into a real one.
- **One-line counter:** *"The law gives families the right to itemized prices.
  We're the ones who actually go get them — and we never steer them to a
  particular home."*

### 7. "Do nothing / the hospice's existing counselors"

- **What it is:** the hospice's own bereavement staff handling the 13-month
  mandate (42 CFR 418.64) with the resources they have.
- **The conflict / gap:** the mandate is **federally required and unfunded**;
  counselors are trained for **grief**, not **funeral-price negotiation** or the
  **after-death admin maze** (certificates, accounts, SSA/VA benefits). It quietly
  drags on **CAHPS** family-satisfaction scores.
- **Why we win:** we cover the **pricing + admin** layer the counselors aren't
  resourced for, **free to the family**, and hand the hospice a **report** that
  proves they delivered the mandate — a CAHPS and compliance asset, not more work.
- **One-line counter:** *"Your counselors do grief beautifully. We do the funeral-
  pricing and paperwork maze they were never resourced for — and hand you the
  report that proves it."*

## The three proof pillars

1. **Conflict-free by design.** We take **no money from funeral homes or
   insurers** and **never charge the family.** Hospices pay. This is structural,
   not a promise — it's the one thing no insurer- or home-funded player can copy
   without breaking its own model.
2. **Real prices, defensible numbers.** We invoke the family's **FTC right** to
   itemized price lists, publish our **methodology**, and never publish a
   home-level figure without **n>5 + significance** (**guardrail #4**). No claim we
   can't defend.
3. **Neutral, never steering.** We present a **side-by-side** and let the **family
   choose** (**guardrail #3** — also the anti-steering law that *lets a hospice
   endorse us*). We deliver the hospice's unfunded mandate, lift CAHPS, save staff
   hours, and prove it in a report.

## Reminders before you use this card

- **Don't bad-mouth competitors.** The distinction is **who pays them and what
  that aligns them to** — not that they're bad. Empathy especially is well-funded
  and genuinely useful; respect it and draw the neutrality line.
- **Keep every figure defensible** (**guardrail #4**). The competitor numbers here
  are sourced and labeled; if a fact is dated, say so. One exposed exaggeration
  undoes the whole "honest" brand.
- **Never imply we take a referral fee or rank homes for money** — we don't, and
  saying we might would break **guardrails #1 and #3** and the hospice's reason to
  trust us.
